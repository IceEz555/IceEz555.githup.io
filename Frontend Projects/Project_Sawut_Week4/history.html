History

Arsenal Football Club has a rich history dating back to 1886. From the early days at Highbury to the modern era at the Emirates Stadium, the club has always been at the forefront of English football. Known for their attacking style of play and tradition of developing young talent.

Key Moments in History

Arsenal was founded in 1886 by munitions workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich. The club was originally named Dial Square before being renamed Royal Arsenal.

The club moved to Highbury in North London in 1913. This stadium would become the spiritual home of the club for 93 years, witnessing many of Arsenal's greatest triumphs.

Under Arsène Wenger, Arsenal completed the entire 38-game Premier League season without a single defeat, earning the nickname "The Invincibles" and a unique gold trophy.

To compete with the biggest clubs in the world, Arsenal moved to the state-of-the-art Emirates Stadium nearby, increasing capacity to over 60,000 spectators.
