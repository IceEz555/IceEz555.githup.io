History of Fashion Week

Fashion Week began in the early 20th century as exclusive events, evolving into a global phenomenon shaping the fashion industry as we know it today.

Origins in Paris

Early 20th Century – Paris couture salon shows introduced formal fashion presentations that laid the groundwork for modern runway shows.

Early 20th Century

Paris couture salon shows

The Rise of NYFW

In 1943, New York held its first organized fashion week, shifting the focus to American designers and marking the start of a fashion revolution.

1943

First Press Week (New York)

Evolution to Global Events

In the 1980s-1990s, Fashion Week expanded to cities like Milan, London, and beyond, becoming a major force in global fashion.

1980s–1990s

Evolution to Global Events

Year/Period Event Impact on Fashion
